BeeGreen - Case Study

The Problem

Many Norwegians in the age group of 25-40 struggle with actively assisting the bee ecosystem due to a lack of awareness, relevant information, and easily accessible resources. This leads them to feel uninformed and powerless in their efforts to contribute and make a positive impact for the bees.

Hypothesis

An accessible platform promoting bee conservation through education and resources will empower Norwegians aged 25-40, leading to increased knowledge, adoption of eco-friendly habits, and a sense of pride in their contribution, thereby positively impacting the bee ecosystem.

Introduction of the solution

BeeGreen is a revolutionary solution that promotes easier access to bee conservation efforts and encourages a more environmentally friendly lifestyle. With bee populations declining rapidly, it is crucial to protect these vital pollinators and embrace sustainable practices.

Through its user-friendly platform, BeeGreen provides educational resources, practical tips, and a community network to empower individuals, communities, and businesses to actively participate in bee conservation and embrace a greener way of life.

Challenges

I aimed to develop an interface that would captivate and engage our target audience, making it easy for them to connect, collaborate, and contribute to the sustainable community.

The usability testing stage in the design thinking process posed challenges, particularly in creating the test screens and ensuring they were error-free. It required attention to detail and multiple iterations. Through research, collaboration, and continuous learning, I overcame these challenges and gained valuable insights for improving the user experience.

Users Impact

The BeeGreen aims to have a positive impact on users, empowering them to take actions that benefit the environment and support bees.

The test screens that I designed and tested with my 3 participants had a positive effect on them, fostering a greater sense of awareness and motivation to make a difference.

Through the app's intuitive interface and engaging features, users were able to learn about the importance of bees, their declining populations, and the critical role they play in our ecosystem. The test screens i used when performing the usability test effectively guided participants to explore various interactive sections, such as planting bee-friendly gardens, identifying bee-friendly products, and participating in local conservation initiatives.

The positive experience and user feedback from the test screens encouraged participants to actively incorporate bee-friendly practices into their daily lives, such as planting bee friendly flowers, avoiding harmful pesticides, and supporting local beekeeping efforts. Overall, the BeeGreen app and the thoughtfully designed test screens inspired users to become advocates for the environment and take meaningful steps to protect bees and their habitats.

Lesson that I have learned

Through this assignment, I gained a comprehensive understanding of the design process from start to finish, including research, planning, and usability testing. I found it educational and enjoyable to work independently and complete each step of the process by myself.

This was the biggest challenge I have had so far in this course. Although I found this assignment to be enjoyable and very rewarding.
